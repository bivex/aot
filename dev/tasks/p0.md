# Task P0: English Subject-Predicate Detection

## Status: Complete (Morphology + Grammar + Frequency-based Disambiguation + Agreement)

The English syntactic module has a complete pipeline: AGID+UniMorph-based morphology with full verb paradigms, a 61-rule GLR grammar, frequency-based homonym disambiguation from UD-EWT, and subject-predicate agreement verification.

### Changes Implemented:

1.  **Morphology (AGID + UniMorph + all.eng)**:
    - Integrated AGID (Automatically Generated Inflection Database) to add regular verb inflections (-s, -ed, -ing, -en) for ~40K verbs via `unimorph_to_aot_v4.py`.
    - Merged with UniMorph English data (~540K lemmas) and `all.eng` base dictionary (pronouns, articles, prepositions).
    - Fixed lemma reconstruction logic to ensure the base form is the lemma (e.g., RUNNING -> RUN).
    - Properly tagged personal pronouns (I=1,sg; HE=3,m,sg; WE=1,pl) to enable agreement checks.
    - Injected closed-class suppletive verb paradigms: **BE** (is/am/are/was/were/been/being), **HAVE** (have/has/had/having), **DO** (do/does/did/done/doing) — tagged as VBE.
    - 17 POS tags: NOUN, ADJ, VERB, VBE, MOD, NUMERAL, CONJ, INT, PREP, PART, ARTICLE, ADV, PN, ORDNUM, PRON, POSS, PN_ADJ.

2.  **Grammar (`simple_syn.grm` — 61 rules)**:
    - NP: bare nouns, pronouns, proper nouns; with articles, adjectives, possessives, numerals.
    - VP: simple verbs, copula (VBE+ADJ/NP/PP), progressive (is running), passive (was broken), perfect (has eaten), perfect progressive (has been running), passive progressive (is being eaten).
    - Modal: can/must/will + verb, + progressive, + passive.
    - Negation: VBE/VOD + not + verb/adj/ing/pp.
    - Infinitive with "to": is to go.
    - Coordination: NP and NP, VP and VP, verb and verb.
    - Root: SYN_GROUP covers NP, VP, PP, ADVERB, PART, SP.

3.  **Subject-Predicate Agreement (`GleicheSubjectPredicate`)**:
    - Implemented logic in `CEngGramTab` to check agreement:
        - 3rd person singular present ("runs", "is") requires 3rd person singular subject.
        - 1st person singular present ("am") requires "I".
        - Past tense "was" vs "were" agreement.
        - Plural "are" requires plural or 2nd person subject.
    - Integrated into `CEngSentence::BuildSubjAndPredRelation` to filter out invalid subject candidates.

4.  **Frequency-Based Homonym Disambiguation**:
    - Generated weights from UD-EWT corpus (201K tokens).
    - Corrected "BOOKS" ambiguity (NOUN/VERB) where NOUN is now preferred due to high frequency.
